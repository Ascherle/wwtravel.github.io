France has long been a global centre of art, science, and philosophy. It hosts the world's fourth-largest number of UNESCO World Heritage Sites and is the leading tourist destination, receiving around 83 million foreign visitors annually. France is a developed country with the world's seventh-largest economy by nominal GDP, and tenth-largest by purchasing power parity. In terms of aggregate household wealth, it ranks fourth in the world. France performs well in international rankings of education, health care, life expectancy, and human development.

France is considered a great power in global affairs, being one of the five permanent members of the United Nations Security Council with the power to veto and an official nuclear-weapon state. It is a leading member state of the European Union and the Eurozone, and a member of the Group of 7, North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO), Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD), the World Trade Organization (WTO), and La Francophonie.

1/11/18
Arrival in Strasbourg

In the early morning I took a bus to the Stuttgart Airport where my friend Ayberk and I got a rental car. He managed to find a really awesome deal and I got to feel fancy for the next few days with an open hatch Fiat. Back in Montana it would be far too cold at this point in the year, but central Europe has a milder climate, I've noticed. From Stuttgart, Strasbourg was a mere 2 hours away by car on the autobahn. After several months using only public transport, I had forgotten how much faster you can get around by car; although the very well developed transport system is something I really appreciate about living in Europe.

Strasbourg City Center

Once we arrived, we checked into our hotel, which was in a small town right outside of Strasbourg. It was sort of the cheapest option we found so it definitely wasn't glamorous, but I've stayed in tents in the mountains for months at a time so it was perfectly fine for me. The first thing we did was take the tram to the city and walk around the city center. The main cathedral, Notre Dame de Strasbourg, is very tall and features a gorgeous rose window on its west facade. For an art history geek, this is a highly recommended place to see! We had dinner at a cozy pub, where I got the most amazing potato cheese gratin and red wine that I have ever tried. The French know how to make food!

2/11/18
Culture & Nightlife

The next day we did a lot of walking around the city. We were determined to find the nicest cafes, so we sat at three different places to people watch. We had coffee at each spot, and it was confirmed that the French also know how to make good coffee. We walked through the market area and I got a huge crepe filled cheese, (again, so much cheese). The lady making them poured a ladle of crepe batter on a flat hot disk and spread it around, creating a proper circular form and waiting for it to become slightly crispy.

Crushed Vintage TV Sets

Next we went to the modern art museum, which has works by abstract, impressionist, and expressionist artists, and many current artists as well. I especially enjoyed seeing art by Kandinsky, Picasso, and Monet. Not every day that you can see all three at once! The modern art was interesting too, lots of themes of everyday items transformed into something thought provoking and weird. For example, a row of old TV sets, some showing random video clips, and the last four with crushed glass. I can't always come up with what the artist must have been thinking, but I like to see such bizarre things and speculate. Another thing that caught my attention was a car covered in toy guns with stuffed children's toys all over the seats. Maybe the artist was commenting on lost innocence? Loss of control? Who knows!

Car filled with Stuffed Animals

We walked to L'Alchemist Bar and had cocktails in a dark and somewhat gothic atmosphere. The drinks themselves had very odd flavours and were served with glow sticks. I think I got something somewhat reasonable, melon flavored vodka, although it was definitely too sweet. We didn't catch the 1 am tram so we spent the rest of our time in another bar drinking wine, eating pizza, and people watching until 3 am, then walking around the city until the tram arrived at 4. Getting up to drive back to Stuttgart the next morning was not so fun!
